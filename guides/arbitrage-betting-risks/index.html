Arbitrage Betting Risks

Arbitrage betting is often marketed as risk-free. In practice, the math can be risk-controlled, but the real-world execution is not risk-free.

  • Odds movement: one side can disappear before you complete all bets.
  • Bookmaker limits: winning or arbing accounts may be limited or closed.
  • Void bets: different rules across bookmakers can break the hedge.
  • Currency and fees: exchange rates and withdrawal fees can erase edge.
  • Legal variation: betting rules differ by country and state.
  • Human error: wrong stake, market or odds format can turn a surebet into a loss.
